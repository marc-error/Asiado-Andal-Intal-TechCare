Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common laptop problems. Search or browse by topic.

Why is my laptop battery draining so fast?
Fast battery drain can be caused by several factors: high screen brightness, background apps consuming power, outdated drivers, or an aging battery. Start by checking your power settings, reducing screen brightness, and closing unnecessary programs. Run a battery health check in Windows settings to see if your battery needs replacement.
How do I fix a laptop that won't turn on?
First, try a hard reset: disconnect power, remove the battery (if removable), press and hold the power button for 30 seconds, then reconnect everything and try again. If that doesn't work, check your power adapter and cable for damage. The issue could also be related to RAM, so try reseating your RAM modules. If none of these work, you may need professional repair.
What causes laptop overheating and how do I prevent it?
Overheating is usually caused by dust buildup in cooling vents, heavy workloads, or operating in hot environments. Prevent overheating by: using your laptop on hard, flat surfaces; cleaning air vents regularly; using a cooling pad; avoiding direct sunlight; and closing resource-intensive programs when not needed. If overheating persists, you may need to clean internal fans or replace thermal paste.
Why is my laptop running so slow?
Slow performance can result from: too many startup programs, insufficient RAM, full hard drive, malware, or outdated hardware. Solutions include: disabling unnecessary startup apps, running disk cleanup, upgrading to an SSD, adding more RAM, running antivirus scans, and updating drivers. Also check if Windows is running updates in the background.
How do I fix a laptop screen that's flickering?
Screen flickering can be caused by incompatible or outdated display drivers, incorrect refresh rate settings, or loose display cables. First, update your graphics drivers. Then check your display refresh rate in Windows settings and try different values. If the problem persists, it might be a hardware issue with the display cable or screen itself requiring professional repair.
My laptop keyboard isn't working. What should I do?
For non-working keyboards, try these steps: restart your laptop, check if keyboard is disabled in Device Manager, update keyboard drivers, test in safe mode, and clean under keys if they're sticky. If specific keys don't work, there may be liquid damage or debris. External keyboards can be used as a temporary solution while arranging repair.
How often should I clean my laptop?
Clean your laptop's exterior and keyboard weekly with a microfiber cloth. Clean air vents every 3-6 months using compressed air. For internal cleaning (fans, heatsinks), do it every 6-12 months depending on your environment. Dusty or pet-friendly homes may require more frequent cleaning. Always power off and unplug before cleaning.
Can I upgrade my laptop's RAM and storage?
Most laptops allow RAM upgrades, and many support storage upgrades from HDD to SSD. Check your laptop's manual or manufacturer website for upgrade options. Ultrabooks and some modern laptops have soldered RAM that can't be upgraded. Storage is usually easier to upgrade - replacing an HDD with an SSD provides the biggest performance boost you can make to an older laptop.
